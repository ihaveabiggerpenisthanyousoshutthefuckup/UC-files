PPPoE Concepts

As shown in the figure, the customer’s router is usually connected to a DSL modem using an Ethernet cable. PPPoE creates a PPP tunnel over an Ethernet connection. This allows PPP frames to be sent across the Ethernet cable to the ISP from the customer’s router. The modem converts the Ethernet frames to PPP frames by stripping the Ethernet headers. The modem then transmits these PPP frames on the ISP’s DSL network.

The figure shows tunneling to create a PPP link over Ethernet. On the left is PC1 with the IP address of 10.1.1.1. PC1 connects to the R1 router labeled customer. R1 connects to a modem labeled 10.1.3.1. Under the modem are two rectangles labeled from left to right as PPP and IP packet. There is an arrow coming out of the IP packet rectangle pointing to the cloud labeled ISP. The modem connects to another modem inside a cloud labeled ISP. The modem is labeled 10.1.3.2. The ISP modem connects to the R2 router also located inside the ISP cloud.
