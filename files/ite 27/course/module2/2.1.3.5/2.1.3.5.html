Configuring Extended VLANs

Extended range VLANs are identified by a VLAN ID between 1006 and 4094. Figure 1 shows that, by default, a Catalyst 2960 Plus Switch does not support extended VLANs. In order to configure an extended VLAN on a 2960 switch it must be set to VTP transparent mode.

Figure 2 shows how to create an extended-range VLAN on the Catalyst 2960 Plus Switch.

The show vlan brief command is used to verify that the VLAN was created, as shown in Figure 3. The output confirms that the extended VLAN 2000 has been configured and is active.

Use The Syntax Checker in Figure 4 to configure and verify Extended VLANs.

Note: A Cisco Catalyst 2960 switch can support up to 255 normal range and extended range VLANs. However, the number of VLANs configured will affect the performance of the switch hardware.
